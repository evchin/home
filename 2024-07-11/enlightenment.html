preface

one of my friends who lives in the south of france told me about one part of buddhist ideology that argues that romantic love between two people is selfish, because that love can supposedly be shared with the rest of the world if one is enlightened. apparently, a person who reaches enlightenment is able to feel the same magnitude of love about everything else in the world, instead of limiting it to just one other person.

i think that is such a hot pile of dog shit. i believe this so strongly that it makes me angry that you'd even say such a thing.

there are millions of poeple out there in the world who suffer and die everyday. and you're trying to tell me, you love each and every one of them like you would love your spouse? what are you doing then, up in a monastery in a mountain? go save your spouse. go die for your spouse.

love is not something you can just say. love is an action and a choice. if you loved someone to the degree that you'd love a lifelong partner, you would die for them.

but it's so interesting how those who are "enlightened" tend to isolate themselves from the rest of society. it makes me curious. go to a place of suffering and tell me how enlightened you are afterwards. take a trip down the i-405 every morning and tell me how enlightened you are.

it is easy to be calm and "loving" (i would argue there is nothing loving about you) when nothing disturbs you. you are alone in the fucking mountains - you better be enlightened, because it doesn't seem like you're doing much else.

it is far more incredible to be in a place of great disturbance and still try your best to be a good person and do good upon others.
